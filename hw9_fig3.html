Latitude vs. Cloudiness in Percent

The results for cloudiness measures around the globe leave me questioning the methodologies of the researchers who define what the "cloudiness" percent actually is. There are very clear regions of consistent measurement: at around 20%, 40%, 75%, and 90%, there are denser groupings of dots across all latitudes.

Although I am not a meteorological expert, it would be very surprising to me if clouds only ever appeared in such precise groupings. Much more likely is that the cloud "density" in the sky is being evaluated rather subjectively, which leads me to question how reliable these results are. Could the grouping around 20% actually be observations covering ranges from something likely 15%-25%? It's hard to make any judgements on the data given these aparent flaws.
